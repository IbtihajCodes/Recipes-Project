Recipe Of Tomahawk Steak

Tomahawk Steak

This grilled tomahawk steak recipe is a showstopper, ideal for sharing with friends and family. This reverse-seared steak is cooked to perfection with an unctuous compound butter filled with shallots, garlic, and chives. I like to use a Bluetooth-enabled meat thermometer to help me achieve the perfect medium-rare internal temperature.

Ingredients:

  • Brown Sugar
  • Montreal Steak Seasoning
  • Garlic Powder
  • Onion Powder
  • Tomahawk Steak
  • Salted Butter
  • Minced Shallot
  • Minced Garlic Clove
  • Fresh Chives
  • Steak Rub

Steps:

  1. Rub brown sugar, Montreal steak seasoning, garlic powder, and onion powder in an airtight container.
  2. Place steak on a half sheet pan, and rub steak on top
  3. Place butter, shallot, garlic, chives, and the reserved 2 teaspoons steak rub in a small bowl.
  4. Remove steak from the refrigerator 1 hour before cooking to take the chill off.
  5. The reverse sear method is used to cook this big steak, so you will need an instant-read thermometer.
  6. Bake steak on the sheet pan in the preheated oven until an instant-read thermometer inserted into the center.
  7. Preheat an outdoor grill for high heat and lightly oil the grate.
  8. Remove steak from the oven.
  9. Grill steak directly on the grates of the preheated grill; sear on both sides.
  10. The internal temperature should now read 125oF to 135oF (51 - 57 oC) for medium rare.
  11. Remove steak from the grill, and smear compound butter over top.
  12. Let butter melt into the steak, heating up the fresh garlic, shallots, and chives. Let steak rest for 10 minutes.
  13. Slice steak and serve with accumulated juices and melted butter.
  14. Be sure to serve the bone, someone will want to gnaw on it!
